Overview

This document outlines the process of replacing the Fuse 1 RFID board. Only perform this procedure if instructed to do so by a Formlabs Support Agent or Authorized Service Partner.

WARNING

Read and follow all safety instructions and warnings in the operator manual. Replacement with incorrect parts can lead to burns, shock, fire, explosion or catastrophic mechanical failure. Dispose of all parts in accordance with local regulations.

NOTICE

The Fuse 1 contains sensitive electronic components. Ground yourself before touching any electronics in the printer by using an antistatic wrist band or touching a grounded piece of metal.

Tools

  • 2.5 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 3 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • T10 Torx driver
  • RFID board (RP-F3L-RFID-01)

1: Remove the top enclosure panel

  1. Shutdown the printer, then unplug the power cable from the rear panel.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the buffer-side.


















  3. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 12x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on top of the printer.










  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.
  1. Lift the top panel off of the printer by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge.
  2. Set the top enclosure panel aside.

2: Remove the original RFID board

  1. Remove the IR sensor, then disconnect the ethernet cable.
  2. Set the IR sensor aside on a flat surface, with the sensor window facing up.
  3. Feed the IR sensor’s ethernet cable through the plastic shroud.
  4. Use a 3 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 8 mm M4 socket head screws securing the upper cooling shroud to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not yet separate the upper cooling shroud from the printer.




  1. Gently lift up but do not separate the front edge of the plastic shroud from the top of the printer so that the cables attached to the shroud can be accessed.
  1. IMPORTANT Take extreme caution when lifting the plastic shroud so as to not strain the cables attached to it.




  1. Disconnect the fan cable from the connector labeled “C047” by squeezing on the plastic latch.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not pull by the wires of the cable; only pull on the plastic connector.
  2. NOTE It may be helpful to have one person hold the plastic shroud while another person unplugs the cables.
  1. Unplug the cable from the RFID board by gently pulling on the white connector.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not pull by the wires of the cable; only pull on the plastic connector.



  1. Lay the upper cooling shroud down on a flat, clean surface, so that the RFID board is facing up.
  2. Use a Torx T10 driver to remove the 2x 10 mm screws securing the RFID board to the upper cooling shroud.
  3. Set the original RFID board aside.

3: Install the new RFID board

  1. Touch a grounded metal surface to discharge any residual static electricity.
  2. Remove the replacement RFID board from its packaging.
  3. Position the new RFID board so that the 2x screw holes just below the connector are aligned with the 2x screw holes on the upper cooling shroud.
  4. Use a Torx T10 driver to replace the 2x 10 mm screws that secure the RFID board to the upper cooling shroud.
  5. Hold the upper cooling shroud above the printer and plug the connector into the RFID board by gently pushing it into the receptacle.
  1. IMPORTANT The RFID board connector is polarized, and will only fit in one orientation.

  1. Reconnect the cooling fan connector to the socket  labeled “C047”.








  2. Reposition the top cooling shroud by holding it from the front edge so that the cables covered by the shroud can still be accessed.
  3. Feed the IR sensor’s ethernet cable back through the cutout in the top shroud.



  4. Use a 3 mm hex driver to replace the 4x 8 mm M4 socket head screws that secure the upper cooling shroud to the printer.









  1. Inspect the IR sensor lens for debris and follow the cleaning documentation if necessary.
  2. Reconnect the ethernet cable to the IR sensor, then replace the sensor puck back onto the printer.

4: Replace the top enclosure panel

  1. Lift the top panel by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge, then align it with the top of the printer.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the front end of the buffer-side.


















  3. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 12x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on top of the printer.




  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.
  2. Reconnect the power cable to the rear panel, then turn the printer on.
