Overview

This document outlines the process of replacing the Fuse 1 touchscreen display assembly. Only perform this procedure if instructed to do so by a Formlabs Support Agent or Authorized Service Partner.

WARNING

Read and follow all safety instructions and warnings in the operator manual. Replacement with incorrect parts can lead to burns, shock, fire, or explosion.

WARNING

This procedure involves relocating the Fuse 1 Frontal Lobe board (the motherboard, referred to as the FoLoBo) to a new display assembly. The FoLoBo is a sensitive circuit assembly and must be treated with care. Any damage from mishandling or exposure to static electricity may render the FoLoBo and thus the printer inoperable.

NOTICE

The Fuse 1 contains sensitive electronic components. Ground yourself before touching any electronics in the printer by using an antistatic wrist band or touching a grounded piece of metal.

Tools

  • 2.5 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 3 mm hex driver (must have a 90º head)
  • ESD-safe spudger (recommended) or ESD-safe tweezers
  • Antistatic wrist band
  • Flashlight (optional)
  • Fuse 1 display assembly (RP-FS1-SN-01)

1: Remove the top enclosure panel

  1. Shutdown the printer, then unplug the power cable from the rear panel.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws closest to the front of the printer on the buffer-side.

















  3. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 12x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws securing the top panel to the printer.











  4. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.
  5. Lift the top panel off of the printer by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge.
  6. Set the top panel aside.

2: Remove the top cooling shroud

  1. Lift off the IR sensor, then disconnect the ethernet cable.
  2. Set the IR sensor aside on a flat surface, with the sensor window facing up.
  3. Feed the IR sensor’s ethernet cable through the plastic shroud.
  4. Use a 3 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 8 mm M4 socket head screws securing the upper cooling shroud to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not yet separate the upper cooling shroud from the printer.




  1. Gently lift up but do not separate the front edge of the plastic shroud from the top of the printer so that the cables attached to the shroud can be accessed.
  1. IMPORTANT Take extreme caution when lifting the plastic shroud so as to not strain the cables attached to it.




  1. Disconnect the fan cable from the connector labeled “C047” by squeezing on the plastic latch.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not pull by the wires of the cable; only pull on the plastic connector.
  2. NOTE It may be helpful to have one person hold the plastic shroud while another person unplugs the cables.
  1. Unplug the RFID reader board cable from the circuit board by gently pulling on the white connector.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not pull by the wires of the cable; only pull on the plastic connector.


3: Remove the original display assembly

  1. Put on an antistatic wrist band, then connect it to a known ground point.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not use any surfaces that are powder-coated or painted.
  2. NOTE If near the Fuse Sift’s vacuum, it is possible to use the collection bin’s grounding cable as a grounding point. Ensure the vacuum is connected to the Fuse Sift, which should be connected to a mains outlet.
  1. Locate the Frontal Lobe board (FoLoBo) mounted behind the display panel glass.
  2. Begin disconnecting the USB, ethernet, Wi-Fi and JST connectors from the FoLoBo shown in the image on the right.
  1. IMPORTANT Never disconnect a connector by pulling on the cable. Always disengage the locking tab, then pull the connector away from the socket by grabbing the plastic housing.
  2. NOTE Use a pair of ESD-safe tweezers to disconnect the 2x (or 1x in newer printers) Wi-Fi coaxial connectors. These connectors are extremely fragile and can easily bend or break if improperly handled.
  3. NOTE After disconnecting the RFID reader cable harness, set it aside. Leave all other cable harnesses inside the top housing.
  1. Open the filter cabinet door, then manually open the print enclosure by pulling the blue release handle.
  2. Use a 3 mm hex driver to remove the 2x 6 mm M4 black socket head screws holding the display assembly to the top of the printer.
  1. NOTE The entire display assembly will be free once these 2x screws are removed. Hold the assembly with one hand when removing the last remaining screw.
  1. Carefully tip the display assembly forward and away from the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT If the assembly becomes caught on something, DO NOT forcibly pull it away from the printer. Check that it is clear of the yellow, black, red and white cable harnesses just above the door handle.
  1. Lay the display assembly glass-side down on a flat, clean surface.


  2. Check that the antistatic wrist band is still connected to a grounding point.
  3. Disconnect the 2x ribbon cables and 1x JST connector (this does not exist on newer printers) from the FoLoBo.
  4. Check that the second coaxial Wi-Fi connector (if applicable) is disconnected from the FoLoBo.
  5. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 8 mm M3 socket head screws securing the FoLoBo to the back of the display assembly.
  6. Carefully pick up and set the FoLoBo aside onto a flat, clean, anti-static surface.
  1. WARNING DO NOT place the FoLoBo onto carpet, stainless steel or non-grounded work surfaces as static discharges may damage the FoLoBo. If the FoLoBo is damaged during this procedure, the entire printer will need to be replaced.

4: Install the new display assembly

  1. Put on an antistatic wrist band, then connect it to a known ground point.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not use any surfaces that are powder-coated or painted.
  2. NOTE If near the Fuse Sift’s vacuum, it is possible to use the collection bin’s grounding cable as a grounding point. Ensure the vacuum is connected to the Fuse Sift, which should be connected to a mains outlet.
  1. Unpack the new display assembly, and lay it glass-side down on a flat, clean surface.
  1. NOTE We recommend laying down the ESD-safe plastic wrap the new display assembly shipped with onto the work surface before proceeding.
  1. Place the FoLoBo into position on the new display panel assembly. Ensure the ribbon cables, JST and coaxial connectors are not trapped beneath the FoLoBo.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 4x 8 mm M3 socket head screws that secure the FoLoBo to the back of the display assembly.
  3. Reconnect the 2x ribbon cables to the FoLoBo.
  4. Reconnect the second coaxial Wi-Fi connector to the lower coax jack labeled “P2”.
  1. NOTE Newer printers and display assemblies no longer have a second Wi-Fi antenna and coaxial connector. If this is the case, leave the P2 connection empty.
  1. Bring the display assembly up to the printer at an angle and align the tab at the bottom with the gap directly above the print enclosure opening.
  2. Ensure that the yellow, back, white and red cable harnesses are not being crushed by the tabs on the back of the display assembly.
  1. NOTE The harnesses should clear the space between the two tabs on the back of the display assembly.
  1. Pivot the display assembly back towards the top of the printer so the display’s flange tabs are flush with the corresponding flanges on the frame.
  2. Replace the 2x M4 socket head screws by hand. Do not yet fully tighten.















  3. Check that none of the harnesses above the door handle are caught between the display assembly and printer’s frame.












  4. Proceed with tightening the 2x 6 mm M4 black socket head screws with a 3 mm hex driver.






  5. Begin reconnecting all 7x JST connectors, the ethernet, USB and Wi-Fi coax cables to the FoLoBo. Refer to the image below for mapping each connector with its socket.
  1. NOTE Newer printers and display assemblies no longer have a second Wi-Fi antenna and coaxial connector. If this is the case, leave the P2 connection empty and reconnect the bulkhead antenna to P1.


5: Replace the top cooling shroud

  1. Hold the upper cooling shroud above the printer and plug the connector into the RFID board by gently pushing it into the receptacle.
  1. IMPORTANT The RFID board connector is polarized, and will only fit in one orientation.


  1. Reconnect the cooling fan connector to the socket  labeled “C047”.








  2. Reposition the top cooling shroud by holding it from the front edge so that the cables covered by the shroud can still be accessed.
  3. Feed the IR sensor’s ethernet cable back through the cutout in the top shroud.


  4. Use a 3 mm hex driver to replace the 4x 8 mm M4 socket head screws that secure the upper cooling shroud to the printer.







  5. Inspect the IR sensor lens for debris and follow the cleaning documentation if necessary.
  6. Attach the ethernet cable to the IR sensor, then replace the sensor puck into its mount.







6: Replace the top enclosure panel

  1. Lift the top panel by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge, then align it with the top of the printer.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the front end of the buffer-side.


















  3. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 12x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on top of the printer.







  4. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.
  5. Reconnect the power cable to the rear panel, then turn the printer on.
