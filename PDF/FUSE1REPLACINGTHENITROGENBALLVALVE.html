Overview

This document outlines the process of replacing the Fuse 1+ 30W nitrogen ball valve.

DANGER

Performing this procedure on the Fuse 1+ 30W while it is plugged in increases the risk of electric shock. Never remove the rear paneling while the Fuse 1+ is plugged in. Never reconnect the Fuse 1+ 30W to power while the rear paneling is uninstalled. After disconnecting the power from the Fuse 1+ 30W, wait at least 5 minutes before servicing.

NOTICE

The Fuse 1+ 30W contains sensitive electronic components. Ground yourself before touching any electronics in the printer by using a grounding strap or touching a grounded piece of metal.

Tools

  • 2.5 mm hex driver
  • 2 mm hex driver
  • T20 Torx driver
  • Slotted screwdriver
  • Teflon tape
  • Adjustable wrench
  • Fuse 1+ 30W nitrogen valve (RP-FS1P-NV-01)

1: Remove the rear enclosure panels

  1. Shutdown the printer, then unplug the power cable from the rear panel.
  2. Remove the Wi-Fi antenna, if present.
  3. Disconnect the N2 hose from the quick-disconnect fitting, if connected.
  4. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 6x black and 4x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws securing the lower rear panel to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.
  1. Lay the lower rear panel down on a flat, clean surface.
  2. Locate the aluminum exhaust duct and the screw for the ring clamp securing the duct.
  3. Use a 6.5mm slotted screwdriver to loosen, but do not remove, the ring clamp screw so that the duct can be pulled away from the exhaust pipe.





  1. Open the filter cabinet door and locate the exhaust inlet on the right-hand side of the cabinet.
  2. Use a 2 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 8 mm M3 countersunk screws flanking the inlet.





  3. With both ends of the exhaust ducting disconnected, use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 2x black and 2x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws from the upper rear panel.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.














  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws that connect the upper rear panel along the buffer, top and hopper sides of the printer.

  1. Pull the remaining rear enclosure panel straight back and away from the printer, then lay it down on a flat, clean surface.

2: Remove the top enclosure panel

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the front end of the buffer-side.




  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on top of the printer.

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.
  2. Lift the top panel off of the printer by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge.
  3. Set the top enclosure panel aside.












3: Remove the valve

  1. Using a slotted screwdriver, loosen the ring clamp and remove the air intake tube at the bottom of the valve.

  1. Disconnect the cable from connector J088 on the nitro board below.

  1. Using a T20 Torx driver and a 2.5 mm hex driver, remove the 3x T20 screws on top and 2x M3 socket head screws/washers on the hopper-side which secure the N2 plenum to the printer.
  1. NOTE At this point, it is possible to simply unscrew the valve and remove it from the printer. However, it may be uncomfortable or difficult to do so, therefore it is recommended to remove the entire plenum assembly prior.
  1. Using a slotted screwdriver, loosen the 2x ring clamps and remove the aluminum tubes. On newer printers, there will be a white rubber gasket underneath the indicated tube. This can remain in place. Remove the silicone pressure tap tube.
  2. Locate the brass elbow fitting towards the rear hopper side of the printer. Push the brass ring down while pulling on the plastic tube to disconnect it.

  1. Remove the plenum assembly from the printer and set on a flat surface.
  2. Either by hand or with an adjustable wrench, unscrew the valve from the rest of the assembly as shown.

4: Install the replacement valve

  1.  If the replacement valve does not come with the plastic barb fitting and the brass straight fitting pre-installed, apply 1-2 turns of teflon tape to the threads in a clockwise motion, if none exists. Use an adjustable wrench to secure the fittings to the valve as shown.
  2. Apply 1-2 turns of teflon tape to the remaining threads of the brass straight fitting in a clockwise motion, if none exists.

  1. Either by hand or with an adjustable wrench, screw the new valve into the plenum assembly until it bottoms out and the gearbox is in the orientation shown.

  1.  Reinstall the plenum assembly with the new valve onto the top rear of the printer.
  2.  Using a T20 Torx driver and a 2.5 mm hex driver, install the 3x T20 screws on top and 2x M3 socket head screws/washers on the hopper-side to secure the N2 plenum to the printer.
  3.   Reattach the silicone pressure tap tube. Reinstall the aluminum tubes and using a slotted screwdriver, tighten the 2x ring clamps.

  1. Locate the brass elbow fitting towards the rear hopper side of the printer. Push the plastic tube firmly into the fitting.
  1. IMPORTANT After insertion, pull on the tube gently to ensure it does not come out.

  1.  Reconnect the cable to connector J088 on the nitro board.

  1.  Reattach the air intake tube to the bottom of the valve and using a slotted screwdriver, tighten the ring clamp.

5: Replace the top enclosure panel

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 4x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the front end of the buffer-side.


















  2. Use a 2.5mm hex driver to replace the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on top of the printer.












  3. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.












6: Replace the rear enclosure panels

  1. Align the upper rear enclosure panel to the printer using the 4x screw holes on the top enclosure panel.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws that connect the upper rear panel along the top of the printer.
  1. NOTE It may be necessary to slightly push aside one or both ends of the exhaust duct to fully re-seat the upper rear panel.

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 2x black and 2x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws for the rear panel.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.









  1. Slide the ring-clamped end of the duct back onto the exhaust pipe so that the duct is pressing up against the insulation panel.
  2. Use a 6.5 mm slotted screwdriver to tighten the ring clamp.

  1. Use a 2 mm hex driver to secure  the other end of the exhaust duct to the back of the filter cabinet. Be sure to firmly re-thread the 4x 8 mm M3 countersunk screws so there is no gap between the duct’s mounting plate and the filter cabinet housing.
  1. IMPORTANT Check the seal of the exhaust duct by shining a flashlight around the inlet inside the filter cabinet. If light is visible behind the filter cabinet, the duct is not properly sealed.
  1. Close the filter cabinet door.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 6x black and 4x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws that secure the lower-half of the rear panel to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.
  1. Replace the Wi-Fi antenna, if previously installed.
  2. Replace the N2 hose to the quick-disconnect fitting, if previously installed.
  3. Plug the power cable back into the rear panel, then turn the printer on.
