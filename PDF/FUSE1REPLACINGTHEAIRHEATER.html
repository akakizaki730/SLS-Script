Overview

This document outlines the process of replacing the Fuse 1+ air heater. Only perform this procedure if instructed to do so by a Formlabs Support Agent or Authorized Service Partner.

DANGER

Performing this procedure on the Fuse 1+ while it is plugged in increases the risk of electric shock. Never remove the rear paneling while the Fuse 1+ is plugged in. Never reconnect the Fuse 1+ to power while the rear paneling is uninstalled. After disconnecting the power from the Fuse 1+, wait at least 5 minutes before servicing.

WARNING

Use appropriate personal protective equipment when handling loose or unsintered powder. Review the support article Safety with Formlabs SLS products for more information.

  • Wear non-reactive nitrile gloves and safety glasses.
  • Handle loose powder only with adequate ventilation.
  • Wear a respirator (N95 or better) while handling powder.
  • Avoid breathing mist/vapor/spray/dust.
  • Do not eat, drink, smoke, or use personal products when handling powder. Avoid contact with skin, eyes, and clothing.

WARNING

Read and follow all safety instructions and warnings in the operator manual. Replacement with incorrect parts can lead to burns, shock, fire, or explosion. Dispose of all parts in accordance with local regulations.

WARNING

Do not perform this procedure if the printer was printing less than 6 hours prior. Failure to do so may result in serious burns. Allow the print enclosure to fully cool to room temperature before touching or inspecting any Fuse 1+ components.

Tools

  • 2 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 2.5 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 3 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 6.5 mm slotted screwdriver
  • T20 Torx driver
  • Utility knife
  • Flush cutters
  • Safety glasses
  • N95 or better respirator
  • Nonreactive nitrile gloves
  • 100 mm (4 in) foil tape
  • Fuse 1+ air heater (RP-FS1P-120-HR-01 or RP-FS1P-230-HR-01)

1: Remove the rear enclosure panels

  1. Shutdown the printer, then unplug the power cable from the rear panel.
  2. Remove the Wi-Fi antenna, if present.
  3. Disconnect the N2 hose from the quick-disconnect fitting, if connected.
  4. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 6x black and 4x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws securing the lower rear panel to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.
  1. Lay the lower rear panel down on a flat, clean surface.


  2. Locate the aluminum exhaust duct and the screw for the ring clamp securing the duct.
  3. Use a 6.5mm slotted screwdriver to loosen, but do not remove, the ring clamp screw so that the duct can be pulled away from the exhaust pipe.





  1. Open the filter cabinet door and locate the exhaust inlet on the right-hand side of the cabinet.
  2. Use a 2 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 8 mm M3 countersunk screws surrounding the inlet.





  3. With both ends of the exhaust ducting disconnected, use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 2x black and 2x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws from the upper rear panel.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.














  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws that connect the upper rear panel along the buffer, top and hopper sides of the printer.

  1. Pull the remaining rear enclosure panel straight back and away from the printer, then lay it down on a flat, clean surface.

2: Remove the top enclosure panel

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the front end of the buffer-side.
















  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on top of the printer.








  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.
  2. Lift the top panel off of the printer by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge.
  3. Set the top enclosure panel aside.












3: Remove the buffer-side enclosure panel

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws that secure the buffer-side panel to the printer.
  2. Lay the buffer-side enclosure panel down on a flat, clean surface.

4: Remove the insulation and inner rear panels

  1. Put on a respirator, a pair of safety glasses and a pair of nitrile gloves.
  2. Use a 6.5 mm slotted screwdriver to loosen, but do not remove, the ring clamp screw securing the duct to the air heater inlet.  
  3. Slide the duct off of the inlet. Gently move it away from the rear insulation panel.
  4. Straighten the 3x metal retention tabs securing the rear upper insulation panel.
  1. IMPORTANT Ensure that both halves of each tab are parallel to each other. If not, they will shred the insulation as the panel is removed.













  1. Using a T20 Torx driver and a 2.5 mm hex driver, remove the 3x T20 screws on top and 2x M3 socket head screws/washers on the hopper-side which secure the N2 plenum to the printer.
  1. NOTE You do not need to remove the plenum assembly; it only needs to be loose to allow clearance for the insulation and access panel to be removed.  
  1. Feel along the insulation for the seams identified in the image. Use a utility knife to cut along these seams.
  1. IMPORTANT You only need to cut through the foil tape securing the insulation panel; the insulation is already cut.
  2. NOTE Alternatively, locate an edge of the existing foil tape and begin peeling it away from the insulation panel. It will still be necessary to cut the top corner pieces of foil tape in order to separate the panel from the printer.
  1. Carefully pull the insulation panel away from the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT Avoid abruptly removing the insulation panel, as it may begin flaking apart.
  1. Use a 3 mm hex driver to remove the 13x 8.0 mm M4 socket head screws that secure the inner rear panel to the printer.
  2. Use a 6.5 mm slotted screwdriver to separate a corner of the inner rear panel from the inner back plate. Ensure that the driver is inserted between the gasket and second black steel plate.
  1. IMPORTANT Avoid gouging the gasketing as much as possible. Replace if damaged.
  1. Continue separating the inner rear panel from the inner back plate without using the screwdriver.
  1. IMPORTANT Ensure that debris from the insulation panels does not become embedded into the gasket, as this can void the seal when the inner rear panel is replaced.
  2. NOTE Depending on when the printer was built, the gasket may be made of a white or black foam material.

Set the inner rear panel aside on a flat, clean surface with the gasketed-side facing up.

5: Remove the original air heater assembly

  1. Locate the bundle of connectors on the buffer side of the printer.
  2. Locate the pair of wires labeled “C003 AIR”. Disconnect the connectors by pulling firmly from either side. Hold the connectors and not the wires while pulling. Use pliers or wiggle the connectors if they are difficult to separate.
  1. NOTE The connectors may be different colors than what is shown depending on when the printer was built and its repair history. The connectors are not color-coded.
  1. Cut the connectors off the 2x tan braided wires using flush cutters or wire strippers.



  2. Pull the air heater wires back through the silicone grommet to free them from the printer’s frame.




  1. Use a 2 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 6 mm M3 button head screws securing the air heater assembly to the printer frame.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not use a ball-end hex driver, as it will strip these screws. Only use a flat-end hex driver.
  2. IMPORTANT Use a telescoping magnet wand to catch the screws and washers once they are unthreaded.
  1. Remove the original air heater assembly.

6: Install the new air heater assembly

  1. Put on a pair of clean nitrile gloves.
  2. Check that both the gasket around the heater assembly and the heater fins are clean.
  3. Align the new air heater assembly and gasket to the 4x screw holes on the rear of the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The heater wires should be oriented to the buffer side.
  1. Use a 2 mm hex driver to tighten the 4x 6 mm M3 button head screws securing the air heater assembly to the printer frame.
  1. IMPORTANT Do not use a ball-end hex driver, as it will strip these screws. Only use a flat-end hex driver.




  1. From the inside of the printer, feed the 2x air heater wires through the rubber grommet on the buffer side of the printer.
  2. Pull any remaining wire slack out.

  1. If the ends of the wires are not stripped, use wire strippers to remove ~10mm of insulation.
  2. Twist the strands of each wire, insert each into their own new spade connector, and use a crimping tool to crimp it onto each wire. Gently tug the wires to ensure they don’t come free.
  3. Connect the spade connectors on the replacement air heater to those on the pair of wires labeled “C003 AIR”.
  1. NOTE The wires on the Fuse 1+ air heater are interchangeable, it does not matter which wire goes to which connector.
  1. Resecure the cables into the cable clamp if necessary.

7: Replace the inner rear and insulation panels

  1. Put on a pair of clean nitrile gloves.
  2. Use a 3 mm hex driver to replace the 13x 8 mm M4 socket head screws to secure the inner rear panel to the machine.






  1. Straighten the 3x metal retention tabs that secure the rear upper insulation panel to the back of the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT Ensure that both halves of each tab are parallel to each other. If not, they will shred the insulation as the panel is removed.
  1. Slide the upper rear insulation panel in place and fold the tabs over to secure it.







  1. Use 100 mm (4 in) foil tape to cover any seams around the rear insulation panel. If the previous tape was peeled off instead of cut, replace the entire piece.





  1. Slide the air heater duct back onto the inlet pipe, then tighten the ring clamp to secure the duct.

  1. Using a T20 Torx driver and a 2.5 mm hex driver, reinstall the 3x T20 screws on top and 2x M3 socket head screws/washers on the hopper-side to secure the N2 plenum to the printer.

8: Replace the buffer-side enclosure panel

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 4x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws to secure the buffer-side panel back onto the printer.

9: Replace the top enclosure panel

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 4x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the front end of the buffer-side.


















  2. Use a 2.5mm hex driver to replace the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on top of the printer.












  3. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.












10: Replace the rear enclosure panels

  1. Align the upper rear enclosure panel to the printer using the 4x screw holes on the top enclosure panel.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws that connect the upper rear panel along the top of the printer.
  1. NOTE It may be necessary to slightly push aside one or both ends of the exhaust duct to fully re-seat the upper rear panel.

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 2x black and 2x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws for the rear panel.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.









  1. Slide the ring-clamped end of the duct back onto the exhaust pipe so that the duct is pressing up against the insulation panel.
  2. Use a 6.5 mm slotted screwdriver to tighten the ring clamp.

  1. Use a 2 mm hex driver to secure  the other end of the exhaust duct to the back of the filter cabinet. Be sure to firmly re-thread the 4x 8 mm M3 countersunk screws so there is no gap between the duct’s mounting plate and the filter cabinet housing.
  1. IMPORTANT Check the seal of the exhaust duct by shining a flashlight around the inlet inside the filter cabinet. If light is visible behind the filter cabinet, the duct is not properly sealed.
  1. Close the filter cabinet door.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 6x black and 4x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws that secure the lower-half of the rear panel to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.
  1. Replace the Wi-Fi antenna, if previously installed.
  2. Replace the N2 hose to the quick-disconnect fitting, if previously installed.
  3. Plug the power cable back into the rear panel, then turn the printer on.
