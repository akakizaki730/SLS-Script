Overview

This document outlines the process of replacing the Fuse 1 doser assembly. Only perform this procedure if instructed to do so by a Formlabs Support Agent or Authorized Service Partner.

WARNING

Use appropriate personal protective equipment when handling loose or unsintered powder. Review the support article Safety with Formlabs SLS products for more information.

  • Wear non-reactive nitrile gloves and safety glasses.
  • Handle loose powder only with adequate ventilation.
  • Wear a respirator (N95 or better) while handling powder.
  • Avoid breathing mist/vapor/spray/dust.
  • Do not eat, drink, smoke, or use personal products when handling powder. Avoid contact with skin, eyes, and clothing.

WARNING

Read and follow all safety instructions and warnings in the operator manual. Replacement with incorrect parts can lead to burns, shock, fire, explosion or catastrophic mechanical failure. Dispose of all parts in accordance with local regulations.

WARNING

Do not perform this procedure if the printer was printing less than 6 hours prior. Failure to do so may result in serious burns. Allow the print enclosure to fully cool to room temperature before touching or inspecting any Fuse 1 components.

NOTICE

This procedure requires completely evacuating all powder from the Fuse 1 hopper, doser and hopper-side flipper trough. Review the tools listed below, as well as the steps for removing all powder from a Fuse 1 before proceeding.

Tools

  • 2 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 2.5 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 2.5 mm hex driver w/ 90º head
  • 8 mm torque nut driver (at least 1.5 N•m)
  • NFPA 652-compliant vacuum, flexible attachment, and brush tools
  • Fuse 1 vacuum cleaning attachment (optional)
  • Safety glasses
  • N95 or better respirator
  • Nonreactive nitrile gloves
  • CA glue (super glue)
  • Fuse 1 doser assembly (RP-FS1-DOSE-01)

1: Empty the hopper, doser & flipper trough

  1. Install a build chamber and optical cassette if they are not present.
  2. On the touchscreen, navigate to Settings > Tools > Empty Hopper.
  3. Follow the instructions in the menu. The printer will automatically deposit the powder from the hopper into the build chamber.
  4. When complete, remove the full build chamber and bring it to a Fuse Sift or Depowdering Kit to recover the powder.
  5. Put on a N95 or better respirator, a pair of nonreactive nitrile gloves and a pair of safety glasses.
  6. Locate and remove the hopper grate (if present) once all powder has been emptied.
  7. Vacuum the remaining powder out of the hopper and doser with either the printed vacuum attachment or other flexible attachment.
  1. NOTE If using the printed attachment, insert it into the hopper such that the plate is flush with the hopper’s bottom lip. Leave it in place with the vacuum running for 10–15 seconds.
  2. NOTE If using a crevice attachment, hold the vacuum for 10 - 15 seconds in various locations to draw out as much powder as possible. Repeat until all visible powder has been removed.
  3. NOTE It may be necessary to stand on a step stool to reach far enough into the hopper to effectively clean the doser.
  1. Remove the vacuum hose and attachments from the hopper.
  2. Open the printer door using the touchscreen.
  3. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 1x 8 mm M3 socket head screw holding the hopper-side quartz tube heater’s filter to the  frame.
  4. Pull the filter forward to remove it from the print enclosure. Set the filter aside.
  5. On the touchscreen, navigate to Maintenance > Motor Moves.
  6. Touch and hold the appropriate directional toggle to move the recoater all the way over to the buffer (left) side of the printer.
  7. Locate the doser and right flipper controls in the Motor Moves menu on the touchscreen.
  8. Home the doser by holding the Home Doser toggle.
  9. Check the hopper (right-hand) flipper trough for any remaining powder by pressing and holding the clockwise (CW) or counter-clockwise (CCW) toggle.

  1. Use a flexible vacuum attachment to vacuum out any remaining powder in the hopper flipper trough. Rotate the flipper to pick up any remaining powder from the trough.
  2. Vacuum up any powder around or near the hopper (right-hand) trough.
  1. IMPORTANT Any remaining powder in the hopper flipper trough or doser will spill out when removing the old assembly. Thus, it is important to clean out as much powder from this area as possible before proceeding.
  1. Touch and hold the Flipper (Right) Home toggle to re-home it.

3: Remove the top & hopper-side panels

  1. Shutdown the printer, then unplug the power cable from the rear panel.
  2. Remove the Wi-Fi antenna, if present.
  3. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 6x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws closest to the front of the printer on the buffer-side.

















  4. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 12x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws securing the top panel to the printer.










  5. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws on the hopper-side that secure the top panel to the printer.
  6. Lift the top panel off of the printer by grabbing the IR sensor door and the rear-most edge.
  7. Set the top panel aside.











  8. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 2x black and 1x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws from the back of the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.









  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 10x M4 (3x 16 mm, 3x 12 mm, 4x 6 mm) countersunk screws holding the hopper-side panel to the printer.
  1. NOTE Remove the top-most 6 mm countersunk screws last to make removing the panel easier.












  1. Allow the panel to tilt away from the printer but do not yet remove it completely.













  2. Locate the black and red cables leading to the interlock switch on the inner left-hand side of the panel that are retained by a cable clamp.
  3. Unlock the cable clamp by pressing the U-shaped tab away from the cables, then deroute the interlock switch cables.
  4. Disconnect the connector labeled “C033” that connects to the interlock switch.
  5. With the interlock switch cables derouted and disconnected, remove the side panel from the printer.
  6. Lay the hopper-side enclosure panel down on a flat, clean surface.
  7. Use a 2 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 10 mm M3 black countersunk screws holding the inner door handle to the printer.
  8. Set the door handle aside.














  9. While grabbing the sides of the hopper shell, carefully lift it upwards then pull it away from the printer.

























  10. Set the hopper shell and lid aside.

4: Remove the hopper

  1. Locate and disconnect the 3x connectors for the hopper LEDs and 2x powder sensors.












  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 4 mm M3 chrome socket head screws securing the hopper to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT If the screws are difficult to drive, firmly press the top of the hopper downward with your hand. DO NOT place any tools or objects directly on top of the hopper, as they may damage the top gasket.

  1. Carefully grab the hopper and lift it straight up towards the top of the printer. Once the bottom gasket has separated from the doser, pull the hopper away from the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT DO NOT remove the hopper by tipping it out and away from the printer. This may damage the gasket and result in powder leaks.












  1. Set the hopper down on its side onto a flat, clean surface.
  1. IMPORTANT DO NOT set the hopper down on either of the gasketed ends or the windowed side with the powder sensors, as these components may become damaged.

5: Remove the original doser assembly

  1. Use a 8 mm nut driver and telescoping magnet wand to remove the 4x 8 mm standoff nuts securing the doser assembly to the build frame.
  1. IMPORTANT There are washers between each standoff nut and the doser. Use the magnet wand to catch the washers as their corresponding nut is removed. Be careful to not drop the washers into the printer, as they will not be recoverable.
  1. Grab the doser assembly and pull it straight back from the printer. Make sure the assembly clears both the locating pin slots and threaded studs.







  2. Disconnect the 2x connectors for the doser motor and encoder.
  3. Set the original doser assembly aside.

6: Install the new doser

  1. Position the new doser assembly onto the printer frame by aligning the 2x locating pins on the doser with the 2x pin slots on the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT Keep the doser parallel to the top of the printer when sliding it into position.
  1. Wiggle the doser assembly until it is seated properly. The doser’s flange face should be below the surface of the insulation and MUST be parallel to the insulation.







  1. Replace the washers, then the standoff nuts as shown.
  1. IMPORTANT DO NOT fully tighten any of the nuts until the next step.
  1. Torque each nut to 1.5 N-m using an 8 mm socket wrench in the sequence illustrated in the diagram on the right.
  1. IMPORTANT Failure to follow this torquing order will result in powder leaks.
  1. Reconnect the connectors for the doser motor and encoder.

7: Doser calibration

  1. Plug the power cable back into the rear panel, then turn the printer on.
  2. Navigate to Settings > Calibration > Doser Calibration.
  3. Follow the instructions on the UI. The printer will automatically find the correct home position for the new doser. The process typically takes up to 30 minutes to complete.
  4. If any errors are encountered during calibration, follow any instructions provided on the UI and re-run the calibration if necessary. Contact a Formlabs Support Agent or Authorized Service Partner if any errors are persistent.

8: Replace the hopper

  1. Shutdown the printer, then unplug the power cable from the rear panel.
  2. Check that the bottom gasket of the hopper is free of debris, such as powder or pieces of insulation.
  3. Carefully grab the hopper and bring it towards the printer.
  4. With the back of the hopper flush with the adjacent insulation panel, guide the hopper straight down onto the doser.
  1. IMPORTANT DO NOT replace the hopper by tipping it in and towards the printer. This may damage the gasket or create an uneven seal and result in powder leaks.











  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 4 mm M3 chrome socket head screws securing the hopper to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT If the screws are difficult to drive, firmly press the top of the hopper downward with your hand while rethreading the first x2 screws. DO NOT place any tools or objects directly on top of the hopper, as they may damage the top gasket.




  1. Reconnect the 3x connectors for the hopper LEDs and 2x powder sensors.

9: Replace the top & hopper-side panels

  1. Use a 2 mm hex driver to replace the 4x 10 mm M3 black countersunk screws that secure the inner door handle to the printer.







  2. Grab the sides of the hopper shell and carefully bring it towards the printer.
  3. Align the shell with the hopper body, then slide it down on top of the hopper.







  4. Align the bottom of the hopper-side panel to the bottom of the printer, then allow it to lean against you.
  5. Reconnect the interlock connector “C033” to its receptacle.
  6. Wrangle the two interlock cables into the cable clamp so the connector is below it.
  7. If necessary, tuck the interlock cable slack into the side of the printer’s frame.





  8. Replace topmost 2x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws into the holes directly below the hopper.
  1. NOTE It may be necessary to loosen these two screws in order to realign the side panel.
  1. Replace the 3x 16 mm M4 countersunk screws into the holes below the filter door.
  2. Replace the 3x 12 mm M4 countersunk screws into the holes above the filter door.



  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 2x black and 1x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws on the back of the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.



  1. Lower the upper enclosure panel straight down onto the machine.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 26x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws to secure the upper enclosure panel to the machine.
  1. NOTE You may need to apply pressure or jiggle the various panels to align the screw holes. It is recommended to loosely position all screws before tightening.


10: Replace the hopper-side heater filter

  1. Align the rails on the filter with those on the printer. Slide the filter back into place.
  1. NOTE When replacing the spatial filter, ensure that the alignment pins slide into their corresponding holes before replacing the screw.
  1. Check that the front of the spatial filter sits flush against the front lamp housing.

  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 1x 8 mm M3 socket headscrew.
  3. Close the print enclosure door.
