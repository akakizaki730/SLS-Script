Overview

This document outlines the process of removing and replacing the Fuse 1+ buffer trough RTD or heater. Only perform this procedure if instructed to do so by a Formlabs Support Agent or Authorized Service Partner.

DANGER

Performing this procedure on the Fuse 1+ while it is plugged in increases the risk of electric shock. Never remove the rear paneling while the Fuse 1+ is plugged in. Never reconnect the Fuse 1+ to power while the rear paneling is uninstalled. After disconnecting the power from the Fuse 1+, wait at least 5 minutes before servicing.

NOTICE

The Fuse 1+ contains sensitive electronic components. Ground yourself before touching any electronics in the printer by using a grounding strap or touching a grounded piece of metal.

Tools

  • 2 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 2.5 mm hex driver (flat-end)
  • 6.5 mm slotted screwdriver
  • Printed cable gland wrench (optional)
  • Pliers (optional)
  • Replacement Fuse 1 generation RTD temperature sensor (RP-FS1-BRTD-02)

OR

  • Replacement Fuse 1 generation trough heater

1: Remove the rear enclosure panels

  1. Shutdown the printer, then unplug the power cable from the rear panel.
  2. Remove the Wi-Fi antenna, if present.
  3. Disconnect the N2 hose from the quick-disconnect fitting, if connected.
  4. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 6x black and 4x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws securing the lower rear panel to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.
  1. Lay the lower rear panel down on a flat, clean surface.


  2. Locate the aluminum exhaust duct and the screw for the ring clamp securing the duct.
  3. Use a 6.5mm slotted screwdriver to loosen, but do not remove, the ring clamp screw so that the duct can be pulled away from the exhaust pipe.




  1. Open the filter cabinet door and locate the exhaust inlet on the right-hand side of the cabinet.
  2. Use a 2 mm hex driver to remove the 4x 8 mm M3 countersunk screws surrounding the inlet.





  3. With both ends of the exhaust ducting disconnected, use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 2x black and 2x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws from the upper rear panel.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.














  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws that connect the upper rear panel along the buffer, top and hopper sides of the printer.

  1. Pull the remaining rear enclosure panel straight back and away from the printer, then lay it down on a flat, clean surface.

2: Remove the buffer trough RTD or heater

  1. From the back of the printer, disconnect the 4x connectors for the buffer flipper motor (C063), encoder (C068), trough heater (C009), and RTD (C029).

  1. Inside the print enclosure, under the buffer trough, use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove the 1x M3 socket head screw and washer securing the RTD.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to remove 2x M3 socket head screws and spring washers.
  3. Thread 1x of the screws into the middle hole and use it as a handle to slide the aluminum bracket towards the rear of the printer. Remove the bracket and set it aside.
  1. If the bracket does not slide easily, use a 2 mm hex driver to partially unscrew the 1x shoulder bolt.

  1. From the rear of the printer, insert the printed tool, pliers, or other similar tool into the access hole while guiding the wires into it.

  1. Unscrew the outer nut of the cable gland and slide it out of the way.

  1. Deroute the wires of the heater or RTD from the rubber seal.
  1. NOTE The rubber seal may be difficult to slide out of the rest of the cable gland. Pliers or a similar tool may be needed to extract it.

  1. Pull either the heater or RTD out through the rear of the printer. It may be necessary to go back to the inside of the printer and push either of them through the hole.
  2. Remove the metal nut that was unscrewed in step 18 and set it aside.

3: Install the buffer trough RTD or heater

  1. Feed the new RTD or heater through the metal nut, and then through the hole in the rear of the printer.

  1. Connect the RTD and heater to their respective connectors to keep them from falling into the printer.

  1. Inside the print enclosure, confirm that both the heater and RTD are hanging under the trough.
  2. Align the heater with groove in the trough; hold with two fingers and guide the heater into the groove.

  1. Reinstall the bracket so that the slot aligns with the shoulder bolt. Push the bracket towards the front of the printer until all the screw holes line up.

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to reinstall the 2x M3 socket head screws and spring washers securing the bracket to the printer.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to reinstall the 1x M3 socket head screw and washer securing the RTD.

  1. At the rear of the printer, reinsert the cables into the rubber seal.

  1. Slide the rubber seal and metal nut forward until they are fully seated in the cable gland.

  1. Disconnect the RTD and heater cables so they do not entangle when tightening the nut.

  1. Use the printed wrench or other tool to tighten the nut by roughly 1 full turn.
  1. IMPORTANT The nut only needs to be snug. If it feels like the wires are getting tangled or the rest of the cable gland starts to rotate, then stop tightening.

  1. Reconnect the 4x connectors for the buffer flipper motor (C063), encoder (C068), trough heater (C009), and RTD (C029).

4: Replace the rear enclosure panels

  1. Align the upper rear enclosure panel to the printer using the 4x screw holes on the top enclosure panel.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 8x 6 mm M4 countersunk screws that connect the upper rear panel along the top of the printer.
  1. NOTE It may be necessary to slightly push aside one or both ends of the exhaust duct to fully re-seat the upper rear panel.

  1. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 2x black and 2x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws for the rear panel.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.









  1. Slide the ring-clamped end of the duct back onto the exhaust pipe so that the duct is pressing up against the insulation panel.
  2. Use a 6.5 mm slotted screwdriver to tighten the ring clamp.

  1. Use a 2 mm hex driver to secure  the other end of the exhaust duct to the back of the filter cabinet. Be sure to firmly re-thread the 4x 8 mm M3 countersunk screws so there is no gap between the duct’s mounting plate and the filter cabinet housing.
  1. IMPORTANT Check the seal of the exhaust duct by shining a flashlight around the inlet inside the filter cabinet. If light is visible behind the filter cabinet, the duct is not properly sealed.
  1. Close the filter cabinet door.
  2. Use a 2.5 mm hex driver to replace the 6x black and 4x chrome 6 mm M4 button screws that secure the lower-half of the rear panel to the printer.
  1. IMPORTANT The placement of the chrome screws is critical to the printer's electrical grounding. Refer to the image on the right for the correct locations of the black and chrome screws.
  1. Replace the Wi-Fi antenna, if previously installed.
  2. Replace the N2 hose to the quick-disconnect fitting, if previously installed.
  3. Plug the power cable back into the rear panel, then turn the printer on.
